Introduction

Bitcoin, introduced in 2009 by an anonymous entity known as Satoshi Nakamoto, has since become a revolutionary force in the world of finance and technology. As the first decentralized cryptocurrency, Bitcoin operates on a peer-to-peer network without the need for intermediaries like banks. Its underlying technology, blockchain, ensures transparency, security, and immutability in transactions. Bitcoin's key feature lies in its limited supply. With a cap of 21 million coins, scarcity is embedded in its protocol, creating a deflationary aspect that contrasts with traditional fiat currencies. This scarcity has contributed to Bitcoin's appeal as a store of value, often referred to as "digital gold."
